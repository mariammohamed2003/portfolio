The Challenge: Most lawyers face difficulties managing their work digitally. They still rely on traditional methods to organize documents, schedule appointments, & communicate with clients, which causes confusion, missed deadlines, & a lack of efficiency. At the same time, many clients don't know how to find the right lawyer or communicate safely & directly. We wanted to solve both problems with one unified online platform.

The Project: We created Law Tech, a responsive web platform designed for both lawyers & clients. Key features included:

  • For Clients: Easy account creation, Search & browse lawyer profiles, Request to contact specific lawyers
  • For Lawyers: Dashboard to manage files & cases, Interactive court map to find nearby courts, Customizable legal templates (petitions, contracts, etc.), Document center to upload, organize, & view legal files, A library of categorized legal books & references, Live chat with clients for direct communication, Smart calendar with reminders for meetings & court dates

Impact: The platform helped bridge the gap between lawyers & clients, & introduced digital solutions to common problems in the legal field. It improved organization, communication, & accessibility to legal services. The project also gave us hands-on experience in team-based development, user experience thinking, & solving real-world issues.

Team: A team of 7 members worked on this project. My role focused on front-end design & layout structure planning. I also contributed to user journey design, page structuring, & content organization.

Detailed Coursework

A deep dive into the academic courses that shaped my knowledge base.

  • Introduction to Archival Science: Overview of archival concepts, history, & practices in managing historical records & institutional memory.
  • Administrative Documents: Focuses on the creation, organization, & lifecycle management of documents in administrative settings.
  • Classification & Indexing of Documents: Teaches methods of categorizing & indexing documents for efficient retrieval & management.
  • Electronic Document Management: Focuses on the systems & tools used for managing digital documents in organizations.
  • Document Metadata: Study of metadata standards & their role in organizing, retrieving, & preserving digital documents.
  • Specialized & Thematic Archives: Examines archives serving specific domains (medical, legal, audiovisual) & their unique practices.
  • Institutional Digital Repositories: Covers the design & management of digital repositories in organizations.
  • Digital Document Access Systems: Tools & methods for making digital records accessible, searchable, & usable online.
  • Digital Conversion of Documents: Techniques & tools for digitizing paper documents & managing born-digital records.

  • Electronic Archiving of Medical Records: Management of digital medical records in compliance with legal & healthcare standards.

  • International Standards for Document Management: Overview of global standards (e.g., ISO 15489) used in organizing & controlling document systems.
  • Medical Records Management & Electronic Archiving: Covers both the practical & regulatory aspects of managing electronic medical records.

  • Operating Systems: Study of operating system concepts such as process management, memory, binary system, file systems, & security.

  • Internet Technologies: Introduces internet architecture, web protocols, & emerging technologies shaping online communication.

  • Concepts of Programming Languages: Introduction to the principles, syntax, & structure of modern programming languages.
  • Computer Programming (1): Introduction to programming logic, control structures, & basic coding using a selected language (C++).
